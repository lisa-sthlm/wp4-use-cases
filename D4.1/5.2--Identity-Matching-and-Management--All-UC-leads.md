## Identity Matching and Management (All UC leads)

**UC6**
-----------------------------
EUDIW improves Identity Matching by enabling secure binding of credentials to a verified digital identity. **Wallet Providers** ensure credentials are linked to the correct individual, while **Relying Parties** can trust that credentials received from certified wallets are valid and associated with that identity. They may also request core identity attributes to match against existing records when needed.
In some cases, full identity matching is unnecessary. For example, verifying a “student” status is sufficient to grant access to a discounted ticket, without identifying the individual. This selective disclosure reduces data sharing while maintaining trust.


**UC7-UC8:**
----------------------------
Currently, there is no formal mechanism for identity matching or verification between a passenger and their ticket. During the booking and purchase process, passengers are not required to authenticate their identity; instead, they must manually enter their personal data and provide physical certificates to prove eligibility for specific conditions, such as disability status for discounted fares or accompanying rights. Once on board, Trenitalia staff must manually verify each passenger's ticket against a physical ID. The introduction of the EUDIW would streamline both the booking phase and on-board identity verification, as passengers could share their credentials digitally, either via the app (remote presentation) or through proximity-based verification, ensuring a more efficient and secure process.



**UC 11:**
--------------------------------
In the current cross-border process, identity is established primarily through paper or scanned documents at each touch-point (MIFI, IRCC, institution, housing, transit operator), with no shared identifier linking the records. Identity matching across those touch-points is manual: each organisation keys the student into its own system from the documents presented, creating multiple unlinked records. Assurance level is implicit rather than explicit — a valid passport, CAQ, and study permit are treated as adequate proxies, with no formal mapping between eIDAS LoA and PCTF levels. Attribute updates (address, programme, status) propagate poorly across organisations, and the withdrawal or revocation of an identity artefact (for example a cancelled CAQ) is not digitally communicated downstream.

The EUDIW-based flow in UC 11 replaces several of these touch-points with a wallet presentation of PID and student EAA, reducing the number of unlinked records, making the assurance level explicit at each presentation, and supporting data minimisation through selective disclosure.
