# 9. Challenges and Opportunities

This chapter steps back from the specifications and reflects on potential pifralls, what is uncertain, and what lies ahead for WP4. Its purpose is to give an honest assessment, distinguishing between **risks** that the consortium has identified and is actively mitigating, and **opportunities** in the form of future directions and open questions that point beyond the current project phase.

The chapter contains the risks and the open questions specific to each use case, drawing on the operational knowledge accumulated during the stock-taking phase and on the consultations recorded in Section 3.2. GRNET consolidated the contributions, removed duplicates, and grouped recurring items to provide a summary at the WP4 level.

Two adjacent and complementary concepts are used in this part of the deliverable, and they are kept distinct deliberately.

* A **risk**, in the sense of Section 9.1, is a *possible adverse event* that may or may not materialise during the implementation or piloting phases of WP4. Each risk is paired with a likelihood, an impact, and an agreed mitigation plan. Risks are drawn from the Grant Agreement risk register and from items surfaced during the stock-taking phase.
* An **open issue**, in the sense of Section 10.2, is a *concrete item awaiting a decision or an action* before or during the implementation phase. Open issues are not hypothetical; they are known gaps or unresolved questions that the consortium must close.

A particular topic may appear in both lists, with its risk dimension treated in 9.1 and its decision dimension treated in 10.2.

### Structure of the chapter

The chapter is organised into two sections, each addressing one closing dimension of the analysis.

* **Section 9.1 — Identified Risks and Mitigation Measures** lists the risks specific to WP4 use cases (drawn from the Grant Agreement risk register and updated with risks discovered during stock-taking), including topics such as interoperability gaps between operators,  instability of technical specifications, regulatory uncertainty in specific Member States, and operational risks at the partner level. For each risk, the section describes the agreed mitigation measures and identifies the owner of the mitigation.
* **Section 9.2 — Future Directions and Open Questions** identifies topics that are relevant to WP4 but are deliberately out of scope for D4.1 or for the current project phase, including extensions to additional transport modes, integration with other Large Scale Pilots, and scalability considerations for production deployment. The section also lists open technical and legal questions that remain unresolved and that will need further work in D4.2 or beyond.
