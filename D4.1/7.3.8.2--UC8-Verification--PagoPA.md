#### Verification

While the issuance of credentials remains a prerequisite, the verification of passenger identity represents the core of UC8. To ensure secure identification, Trenitalia requires the **verification of the PID or DTC** stored within the EUDI Wallet, provided by PagoPA.

To ensure seamless system interoperability, Trenitalia, acting as the Relying Party, performs these verifications in compliance with the **OpenID4VP** standard. This framework enables two distinct types of verification, ensuring that only necessary data is shared through Selective Disclosure.

_Scenario Execution_
UC8 focuses on a two-step verification process, occurring both during the booking phase and on board the train:
- **Remote Verification** (Booking Phase): During the ticket purchase within the Trenitalia app, the passenger initiates a remote sharing process via their EUDI Wallet. By providing a Verifiable Attestation of their PID or DTC, the platform automatically validates their identity. This allows the system to offer specific services, such as reserving a women-only cabin when requested and eligible.
- **Proximity Presentation** (On-board): On the day of travel, Trenitalia staff performs an identity check. The passenger presents their QR-code via the EUDI Wallet to the train conductor’s verification tool. This offline/proximity interaction ensures that the person on board matches the identity verified during the booking phase.

Note: This dual-verification flow (remote + proximity) ensures a high level of trust and security throughout the entire travel experience.
