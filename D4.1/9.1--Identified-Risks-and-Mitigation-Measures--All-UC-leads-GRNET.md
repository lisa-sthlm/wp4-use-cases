## Identified Risks and Mitigation Measures (All UC leads → GRNET)

### SEDIT-X UC
The SEDIT-X use case introduces a complex, multi-domain operational and technical environment involving wallets, issuers, verifiers, payment services, biometrics, and legacy systems across transport, hospitality, and academic-access domains. As a result, the pilot is exposed to a set of interoperability, operational, regulatory, and implementation risks that need to be actively managed throughout specification, integration, testing, and piloting.

The main identified risks and the corresponding mitigation measures for UC3 are summarised below.

#### Cross-sector interoperability risk

SEDIT-X spans multiple service domains, namely aviation, urban mobility, ferry transport, hospitality, and academic access. These sectors operate with different data models, operational procedures, trust assumptions, and technical environments. This creates a risk that the end-to-end traveller journey cannot be implemented consistently across all participating environments.

**Mitigation measures**
- Define a common wallet-based interaction pattern across all episodes, based on standard issuance and verification flows.
- Use the EUDIW Intermediary Service as a reusable integration layer between wallet protocols and sector-specific systems.
- Keep each episode aligned with the common SEDIT-X architecture while allowing controlled domain-specific adaptations.
- Validate interoperability assumptions incrementally through WP4 testing activities and end-to-end pilot rehearsals.

#### Integration with heterogeneous legacy systems

Several participating back-end systems, such as airline DCS environments, airport kiosks, ferry ticketing platforms, hotel CRS/PMS systems, and university IAM solutions, are legacy or semi-closed systems with limited support for modern wallet protocols and APIs. This creates a risk of delayed integration, partial functionality, or dependence on manual workarounds.

**Mitigation measures**
- Introduce adapter layers and façade services to bridge between EUDI Wallet protocols and existing sector systems.
- Rely on the ITB to test reference integration patterns before deployment in partner environments.
- Apply progressive onboarding, starting from simpler or more controlled integration points.
- Where needed, use intermediary or coordination components to decouple wallet interactions from legacy back-end constraints.

#### Protocol and profile variability

Differences in the interpretation or implementation of wallet-related protocols and profiles may result in interoperability issues between issuers, wallets, verifiers, and service providers. Examples include differences in request structure, cryptographic options, supported credential formats, or verification policies.

**Mitigation measures**
- Apply strict conformance and interoperability testing through the WP4 Interoperability Test Bed.
- Agree scenario-specific implementation constraints and profile choices early in the pilot.
- Use automated regression testing to detect incompatibilities as soon as possible.
- Keep the pilot aligned with the applicable EUDI Wallet and OpenID-based specifications used across the project.

#### Overall mitigation approach

Across all identified risks, the main mitigation strategy for SEDIT-X is based on three principles:

1. **Common architecture with controlled variation**  
   A shared wallet-based architecture is maintained across episodes, while allowing domain-specific implementation differences where necessary.

2. **Intermediary-enabled integration**  
   The EUDIW Intermediary Service is used to reduce complexity at service touchpoints and to isolate legacy-system constraints.

3. **Progressive conformance and interoperability testing**  
   Risks are addressed early through the WP4 Interoperability Test Bed, reference implementations, and regression testing.

### UC6
UC6 success depends on managing:
- **Technical challenges** related to the interoperability between actual academic systems and modern identity protocols. Specifically, the main risk is related to the interaction between OpenID4VC protocols with pre-existing procedures and databases at the participating universities (Polimi, Angers) and service providers. As mentioned in paragraph 5.1, the current approaches for the identity verification are limited to national contexts or even fragmented between national universities.
   - _Mitigation_: A preliminary technical preparation phase will be conducted to map data flows and ensure seamless integration. This includes pre-testing the recognition of specific European Student Card attributes within the WP4 Interoperability Test Bed to prevent failures during the pilot.
- **Identity Matching and Attribute Recognition**  to correctly validate the "Student" status abroad through the Student Card. This may result in the system failing to recognize the student's eligibility for services in a foreign Member State.
   -  _Mitigation_: Functional and Interoperability tests will specifically validate the "Identity Matching" process to ensure that ESC attributes are correctly parsed and accepted by international service endpoints.
- **Cross-Border Identification Risks** for specific cities or regions where Airweb do not operate. Failure to properly map these locations could prevent the correct application of local student discounts.
   - _Mitigation_  : UC6 will be piloted in specific areas so as to ensure the Relying Party (Airweb) can programmatically confirm the cross-border eligibility of the student.
- **Proximity Verification Failures** through proximity tools (e.g., at campus gates or on-site terminals) presents a risk of hardware/software incompatibility or signal interference.
   - _Mitigation_: Functional testing will be conducted in order to ensure systems reliability and avoid issues. 

### UC7 - UC8
- **Identity Matching** between reservation name and PID data during both the online booking process and the proximity verification phase. Discrepancies between digital wallet data and ticketing databases could lead to unauthorized access or denial of legitimate discounts.
   -  _Mitigation_: The verification logic will include strict identity-matching algorithms to synchronize the PID attributes provided by the EUDI Wallet with Trenitalia’s reservation records. This ensures that the holder of the discount/ticket is the same individual identified by the wallet.
- **Cross-Border Dimension:**  UC8 needs to be piloted in specific train fares (e.g. Rome-Monaco) to ensure cross-border dimension and multiple identity verification.
   - _Mitigation_: The pilot will specifically target cross-border routes that require mandatory identity verification.
- **Technical Challenges** in interfacing modern OpenID4VC protocols with Trenitalia’s pre-existing ticketing and reservation databases. .
   - _Mitigation_: Trenitalia and PagoPA will cooperate to integrate their architectures in a pre-production system, in compliance with Aptitude
 Standards and protocols. Specific Testing procedures will ensure the UCs liability, the controlled environment is designed to identify and resolve any technical bugs or protocol misalignments before the live pilot, ensuring a smooth UX and a high success rate for credential presentations.
