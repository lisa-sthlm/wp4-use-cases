## Identified Risks and Mitigation Measures (All UC leads → GRNET)

**UC6**
--------
UC6 success depends on managing:
- **Technical challenges** related to the interoperability between actual academic systems and modern identity protocols. Specifically, the main risk is related to the interaction between OpenID4VC protocols with pre-existing procedures and databases at the participating universities (Polimi, Angers) and service providers. As mentioned in paragraph 5.1, the current approaches for the identity verification are limited to national contexts or even fragmented between national universities.
   - _Mitigation_: A preliminary technical preparation phase will be conducted to map data flows and ensure seamless integration. This includes pre-testing the recognition of specific European Student Card attributes within the WP4 Interoperability Test Bed to prevent failures during the pilot.
- **Identity Matching and Attribute Recognition**  to correctly validate the "Student" status abroad through the Student Card. This may result in the system failing to recognize the student's eligibility for services in a foreign Member State.
   -  _Mitigation_: Functional and Interoperability tests will specifically validate the "Identity Matching" process to ensure that ESC attributes are correctly parsed and accepted by international service endpoints.
- **Cross-Border Identification Risks** for specific cities or regions where Airweb do not operate. Failure to properly map these locations could prevent the correct application of local student discounts.
   - _Mitigation_  : UC6 will be piloted in specific areas so as to ensure the Relying Party (Airweb) can programmatically confirm the cross-border eligibility of the student.
- **Proximity Verification Failures** through proximity tools (e.g., at campus gates or on-site terminals) presents a risk of hardware/software incompatibility or signal interference.
   - _Mitigation_: Functional testing will be conducted in order to ensure systems reliability and avoid issues. 

**UC7 - UC8**
------
- **Identity Matching** between reservation name and PID data during both the online booking process and the proximity verification phase. Discrepancies between digital wallet data and ticketing databases could lead to unauthorized access or denial of legitimate discounts.
   -  _Mitigation_: The verification logic will include strict identity-matching algorithms to synchronize the PID attributes provided by the EUDI Wallet with Trenitalia’s reservation records. This ensures that the holder of the discount/ticket is the same individual identified by the wallet.
- **Cross-Border Dimension:**  UC8 needs to be piloted in specific train fares (e.g. Rome-Monaco) to ensure cross-border dimension and multiple identity verification.
   - _Mitigation_: The pilot will specifically target cross-border routes that require mandatory identity verification.
- **Technical Challenges** in interfacing modern OpenID4VC protocols with Trenitalia’s pre-existing ticketing and reservation databases. .
   - _Mitigation_: Trenitalia and PagoPA will cooperate to integrate their architectures in a pre-production system, in compliance with Aptitude
 Standards and protocols. Specific Testing procedures will ensure the UCs liability, the controlled environment is designed to identify and resolve any technical bugs or protocol misalignments before the live pilot, ensuring a smooth UX and a high success rate for credential presentations.
