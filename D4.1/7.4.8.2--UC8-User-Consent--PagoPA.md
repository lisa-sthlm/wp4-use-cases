#### User Consent
User Consent is required in every step of the verification process, asking the passenger for an authentication by PIN or biometrics. The Wallet interface transparently displays the specific attributes requested by Trenitalia, ensuring the user is fully informed. Explicit consent must be granted for each individual transaction, typically finalized through biometric authentication directly on the user's device. 

In UC8, User Consent is a mandatory cornerstone of the verification process, ensuring that the passenger maintains full sovereignty over their personal data. The workflow is designed to be transparent and secure, following these key principles:
- **Transparency**: before any data is transmitted, PagoPA EUDIW interface displays a clear and transparent summary of the specific attributes requested by Trenitalia. This allows the passenger to review exactly which data points such as identity attributes or the "companion entitlement" are being shared, reinforcing the principle of Selective Disclosure.
- **Active Authorization**: explicit consent must be granted for every individual transaction. The process is never automated or hidden; the passenger must actively authorize the release of their Verifiable Attestations through a secure handshake between the Wallet and the Trenitalia app.
- **Strong Authentication:** to finalize the verification, the user must authenticate themselves using a specific method. Depending on the device settings and the user's preference, this is typically achieved through:
  -   Biometric Authentication: Such as Fingerprint or FaceID, ensuring that only the legitimate owner of the Wallet can authorize the data sharing.
  -   Secure PIN: A personal identification number as a fallback or alternative to biometrics.
- **Binding Consent to the Transaction**: By authenticating directly on their device, the passenger creates a secure link between their identity and the specific booking request. This ensures that the applied discounts or the reserved services are tied to a verified and consensual exchange of information.
