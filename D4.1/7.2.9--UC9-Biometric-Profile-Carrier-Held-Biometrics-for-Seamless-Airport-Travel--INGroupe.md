### UC 9: Biometric Profile - Carrier Held Biometrics - for Seamless Airport Travel (IN Groupe)
In UC9 case, the PID (Personal Identification Data) credential is the foundational identity credential held by the traveller in the EUDI Wallet. In this use case, the PID is the primary trust anchor for identity assurance and is used to support identity verification, the binding of travel-related credentials to the same subject, and the selective disclosure of only those personal attributes that are necessary in each operational context.

The traveller will need a valid PID stored in his wallet before the journey begins. In this sense, PID is a precondition for the use case. The broader UC description explicitly treats PID as the root identity credential of the journey and as the basis on which additional credentials and service interactions rely.

The traveller will also need a valid Digital Travel Credential, a topic piloted in WP3.
