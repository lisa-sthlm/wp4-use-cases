## 6.2 Sector-Specific Regulations

Beyond the horizontal legal framework of eIDAS 2.0 (Section 6.1), every WP4 use case is also subject to a body of sector-specific or domain-specific regulation that applies because of the sector in which the use case operates. This section inventories that body of regulation, organises it into three subsections (hospitality, transport, student mobility), and notes a small number of transversal instruments that apply across several sectors.

### How this section was assembled

The content of this section is drawn from the **consolidated list of regulations** contributed by the 11 UC leads during the T4.2 stock-taking phase. The list was initiated by WP2 and maintained as a set of spreadsheets in NextCloud. It is the authoritative source for the regulatory instruments that UC leads consider applicable to their use cases. The list records the regulating authority, the reference of the text, its nature (regulation, directive, implementing regulation, international standard, national programme), its publication status, a link to the text, and a coded assessment of how the instrument relates to credential issuance, credential presentation, credential format, trust models, legal value, data protection, cybersecurity, and sovereignty considerations.

The following subsections group these entries by sector, and relate them to the WP4 use cases. Where the consolidated list does not contain an entry for a sector the gap is flagged explicitly rather than filled with speculative content; the resolution of the gap is carried over to Section 10.2 (Open Issues).

### How the material is structured

The section is organised into three sector subsections, mirroring the three WP4 scenarios:

* **Section 6.2.1 — Hospitality and Guest Registration** addresses the regulatory environment of hotel booking, identity verification at check-in, and national guest-registration obligations.
* **Section 6.2.2 — Transport and Passenger Rights** is by far the richest of the three, because the consolidated list is dominated by instruments that govern air travel, aviation security, border controls, and advance passenger information exchange. It also covers the maritime-specific instrument that applies to ferry scenarios.
* **Section 6.2.3 — Student Mobility and Academic Recognition** addresses the regulatory environment of cross-border student mobility.

### Transversal instruments referenced in the consolidated list

In addition to the sector-specific instruments, the consolidated list includes a small number of **transversal instruments** that apply across several WP4 use cases. These are treated here, in the introduction to Section 6.2, because they do not belong primarily to any single sector.

* **Regulation (EU) 2016/679 — General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).** Applies to every WP4 use case that processes personal data, which is, in practice, every WP4 use case. The consolidated list highlights its relevance to credential issuance, its partial relevance to credential presentation, and its strong connection to the legal value of credentials. It also flags specific provisions that are particularly pertinent to WP4, notably the special-category rules for biometric data (Article 9), the DPIA obligation (Article 35), and the principles of purpose limitation and data minimisation (Article 5). UC 9 (biometric profile for seamless airport travel) and the biometric episode of UC 3 are the use cases where the biometric provisions of the GDPR weigh the most.
* **Directive (EU) 2022/2555 — NIS2 Directive.** Applies to the cybersecurity obligations of essential and important entities, a category that can include transport operators, airport operators, and digital infrastructure providers involved in WP4. The consolidated list records NIS2 as partially relevant to credential issuance and to trust models, and as fully relevant to the cybersecurity posture of WP4 actors. NIS2 shapes the incident-reporting expectations that relying parties and credential issuers in WP4 will need to align with.
* **Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 — AI Act.** Applies to AI systems used for biometric identification, among other categories. UC 9 (carrier-held biometrics for seamless airport travel) and the biometric episode of UC 3 are again the primary WP4 touchpoints. The consolidated list records the AI Act's relevance to data protection (partial) and cybersecurity (yes), and notes transparency and control obligations that apply depending on the specific use of biometric identification.
* **Directive (EU) 2015/2366 — PSD2 (Payment Services Directive 2).** Applies to WP4 use cases that include a payment step in collaboration with WP6, notably UC 3 (urban mobility, ferry episodes), UC 4 (optional payment), UC 6 (student-fare purchase), UC 7 (discounted train fare purchase), UC 8 (train and hotel payment), and UC 5 (optional event payment). The consolidated list records PSD2 as partially relevant to credential issuance and to trust models, and as fully relevant to the legal value of credentials used for Strong Customer Authentication. The main body of the PSD2 analysis is carried by WP6 in D6.1; WP4 inherits PSD2 obligations at the payment touchpoints of the affected use cases.

These four transversal instruments are referenced again, where relevant, within the sector subsections below, without being redefined each time.

### Border-control instruments and advance passenger information

A second cluster of instruments in the consolidated list is sector-agnostic, since it covers border controls and advance passenger information in a cross-cutting manner. This cluster includes:

* Regulation (EU) 2016/399 — **Schengen Borders Code**;
* Regulation (EU) 2017/2226 — **Entry/Exit System (EES)**;
* Regulation (EU) 2018/1240 — **ETIAS (European Travel Information and Authorisation System)**;
* Regulation (EU) 2025/12 — **advance passenger information (APIS) for external border checks**, repealing Directive 2004/82/EC;
* Regulation (EU) 2025/13 — **APIS for the prevention, detection, investigation and prosecution of terrorist offences and serious crime**.

These instruments are most directly relevant to the air-travel use cases of WP4 (UC 1, UC 2, UC 9, and Episode 1 of UC 3). They are therefore treated inside Section 6.2.2, alongside the aviation-specific instruments, rather than in a separate border-control subsection. Where they also apply at maritime touchpoints (for example, APIS at ferry boarding), the relevance is flagged within 6.2.2 as well.

### National instruments

The consolidated list contains, at this time, a single national instrument: the **Spanish National Civil Aviation Security Programme** (*Resolución de 22 de julio de 2021, de la Secretaría General de Transportes y Movilidad*, BOE-A-2021-14195). This instrument applies to the Spanish airport touchpoints of the relevant air-travel use cases. It is cited in Section 6.2.2.

The consolidated list does not, at this time, include national instruments for other Member States involved in WP4 (France, Italy, Greece, Germany, the Netherlands). UC leads are expected to complete the national-law inventory during the late phase of T4.2 and the early phase of T4.3. The gap is recorded in Section 10.2.

### Structure of the subsections

With the transversal instruments and the border-control cluster now placed, the three subsections that follow address the sector-specific regulatory environment of hospitality (6.2.1), transport and passenger rights (6.2.2), and student mobility (6.2.3). Each subsection cross-references the relevant WP4 use cases, so that a reader can move directly from a legal instrument to the use cases it constrains, and back.
