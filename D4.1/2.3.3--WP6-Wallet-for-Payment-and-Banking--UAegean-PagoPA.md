### WP6 — Wallet for Payment and Banking

**WP6** develops the wallet-based payment and Strong Customer Authentication capabilities that some WP4 use cases rely on at their payment touchpoints. WP4, not designing payment mechanisms of its own, consumes the WP6 outputs and propagates the resulting payment evidence to its service-side systems so that the surrounding operational decision (boarding, ticket issuance, service fulfilment) can be taken. 

**WP4 use cases with payment touchpoints**

Several WP4 use cases include a payment step that hands off to WP6. The two subsections that follow cover the cases where the payment dimension is most central to the user journey:

* **UC 3 — Seamless Digital Traveller Experience (SEDIT-X)**, where wallet-based payment is exercised at airport excess-baggage drop-off and at ferry-ticket purchase (including the case where the booking includes a vehicle, with the mVRC providing the booking-context evidence), with an optional taxi-payment scenario subject to PSP support.
* **UC 7 \& 8 — Accessing Discounted Train Fares via EUDIW and Overnight Hospitality and Cross border Train Journey**, where eligibility verification with the European Disability Card is followed by a wallet-mediated payment authorisation using Strong Customer Authentication issued by Bancomat. UC 7's specification also discusses the closely related UC 8 (Overnight Hospitality and Cross-Border Train Journey) flow, in which identity verification (gender attribute, women-only cabin reservation) precedes the same kind of payment step.

Other WP4 use cases also touch the WP6 payment flows at varying levels of intensity (notably UC 6 for student-fare ticket purchase, and the optional payment dimensions of UC 4 and UC 5). These hand-offs are recorded in the corresponding UC specification sheets in **Annex A**.



**Design principle shared across the WP4 payment touchpoints**

A common design principle applies to every WP4 use case that invokes a WP6 payment flow: the wallet remains the **user-facing authorisation channel**, the payment flow is **bound to the transaction context**, and the resulting **payment evidence** is propagated to the service-side systems so that the next operational step can proceed. The wallet is not meant to replace the various "payment actors": the airline, the ferry operator, the rail operator, the hotel, the merchant, or the Payment Service Provider. It complements them by adding a more trusted and user-controlled authorisation channel into flows where identity, entitlement, and transaction details are already part of the user journey.
