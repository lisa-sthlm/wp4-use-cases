# 2. Introduction

This chapter introduces the deliverable, describing briefly what D4.1 is, why it exists, its place within the APTITUDE project, and how it should be navigated. It is written by GRNET, in its capacity as WP4 lead, and is intended to help readers navigate all that follow.

The chapter responds to four main  questions:

* *What is this deliverable about, what is in and out of its scope?* This is answered in **Section 2.1** (Purpose and Scope of this Deliverable), which positions D4.1 as the outcome of Task T4.2 (Stock-taking and Specifications), defines its in-scope content (use case specifications, business requirements, legal and regulatory requirements, solution design, interoperability requirements) and its out-of-scope content (implementation details deferred to D4.2; piloting results deferred to D4.3; wallet development carried out outside WP4). It also explains the deliberate progression from this deliverable to D4.2 and D4.3.
* *How does it connect to the Grant Agreement and to the WP4 objectives?* This is answered in **Section 2.2** (Relation to the Grant Agreement and WP4 Objectives), which recalls the WP4 objectives as stated in the Grant Agreement, names the three WP4 scenarios (Streamlined Travel, Hotel Check-In, Student Access), maps the four WP4 tasks (T4.1 Coordination, T4.2 Stock-taking and Specifications, T4.3 Implementation Support, T4.4 Piloting and Validation) to the three WP4 deliverables, and explains how we are ensuring the WP4
work meets the necessary standards (WP2 technical standards, the EU toolbox, the EUDIW reference implementation).
* *How does it relate to the other Work Packages and to the deliverables that surround it?* This is answered in **Section 2.3** (Relation to Other Work Packages and Deliverables), which places WP4 within the eight Work Packages of APTITUDE, focuses on the three Work Packages with which WP4 has direct operational dependencies (WP2 for technical standards and trust infrastructure, WP3 for Digital Travel Credentials, WP6 for the PBEE Framework), and notes the parallel stock-taking deliverables D3.1, D5.1, and D6.1.
* *How is the deliverable organised?* This is answered in **Section 2.4** (Document Structure), which provides a chapter-by-chapter reader's guide.

A fifth subsection, **Section 2.5** (Terminology and Definitions), defines the key terms used throughout the document (EUDIW, PID, EAA, QEAA, DTC, RP, CI, ARF, among them), and refers to **Annex C (Glossary)** for the full glossary.
