### UC 7: Accessing Discounting Train Fares via EUDIW (PagoPA)
UC7 aligns with the ARF by integrating all primary actors within the Wallet ecosystem. 

First of all, users, specifically passengers in this context, are able to obtain a Wallet Instance directly through a Test App (PagoPA EUDIW). This Wallet serves as a functional reproduction of the official Italian IT Wallet, which is currently defining its components to ensure full compliance with the eIDAS regulation and framework. In this Use Case, PagoPA provides an equivalent wallet that mirrors the official version in terms of components and references, yet operates within a pre-production environment. Consequently, this UC utilizes mock data for the testing phase, although the underlying processes remain authentic and verifiable.

The Wallet Instance is stored directly on the user's device following the Local WSCD model. It is enabled by the official PID and Credential Issuer, Istituto Poligrafico e Zecca dello Stato (IPZS), which issues credentials to the PagoPA EUDIW by interconnecting their respective pre-production frameworks. For this reason, the issuer maintains full responsibility for the authenticity and validity of the credentials throughout the lifecycle.

Finally, the Relying Party is represented by Trenitalia (TRIT), which is responsible for verifying the validity of the presented credentials, specifically the PID and the European Disability Card, to accurately apply discounts during the ticket purchase phase. The implementation of UC7 is governed by ARF standards and OpenID4VC protocols, which facilitate the secure issuance, presentation, and verification of identity and verifiable credentials.
