### UC 1: Board the flight (Amadeus)

#### Overview

The *Board the Flight* use case involves a broad set of stakeholders across aviation, digital identity, trust infrastructure, technical implementation, and end‑user operations. Stakeholder involvement has been organised to ensure that operational, regulatory, and interoperability aspects are consistently addressed throughout the use‑case lifecycle.

#### Stakeholder Categories

At category level, the stakeholders involved or consulted include the following:

#### 1. Aviation Stakeholders

**Airlines**
- **Finnair**, representing non‑EU and Schengen flight operations
- **Lufthansa Group (LHG)**, representing large multi‑hub European airline operations and intra‑Schengen scenarios

**Airports**
- **Helsinki Airport**, supporting non‑EU and EU departure flows
- **Madrid Airport** and **Barcelona Airport**, representing intra‑Schengen departure environments and Spanish operational practices

These stakeholders contribute operational input related to check‑in, boarding, passenger identity verification, and compliance with aviation security and travel regulations.

#### 2. Wallet and Trust‑Infrastructure Stakeholders

This category includes stakeholders involved in the **European Digital Identity Wallet (EUDIW)** ecosystem:

- **Member State EUDIW initiatives**, depending on national EUDIW readiness and deployment timelines up to **2027**
- **PID and DTC attestation issuers**, responsible for issuing authoritative PID and DTC attestations
- **Private EUDIW providers and intermediary Service providers**, supporting interoperability, routing, and trust establishment between wallets and relying parties

These stakeholders provide input on wallet capabilities, PID availability, issuance models, and trust‑infrastructure constraints.

#### 3. End‑User and Operational Stakeholders

- **Travellers**, as end users of digital credentials
- Airline and airport **operational staff**, including check‑in and boarding agents

These stakeholders contribute feedback on usability, passenger experience, and operational practicality in real airport environments.

#### Engagement Methods

Stakeholder engagement for the *Board the Flight* use case is organised through a combination of structured and continuous collaboration methods, including:

- **Monthly working sessions**, involving use‑case leads and core stakeholders
- **Focused workshops and dedicated working sessions** on specific topics such as PID usage, DTC flows, and regulatory alignment
- **Alignment meetings with other use‑case partners** to ensure consistency and reuse across WP‑level activities
- **Bilateral calls**, when needed to address airline‑ or airport‑specific operational topics
- **Common team communication channels**, enabling continuous discussion and information exchange
- **Shared SharePoint repository**, used to store documentation, working materials, and agreed outputs

These engagement methods ensure that stakeholder input is captured iteratively and reflected in both the design and validation of the *Board the Flight* use case.
