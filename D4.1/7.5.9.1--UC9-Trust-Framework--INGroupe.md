#### Trust Framework
In UC9, the Trust Framework defines the common rules and technical standards that enable airport actors to trust identity and travel credentials issued by EU countries (for example, Italian or French passports, national IDs, residence permits and visas); under this framework, relying parties—airlines, security checkpoints, border control and gate agents—can verify traveller identity and travel entitlements because issuers are recognised as authorised national authorities, allowing check in, security screening, boarding and entry procedures to rely on digitally verifiable attestations issued across Member States; additionally, the framework covers verification of information provided by airlines on boarding passes (flight number, seat assignment, boarding group, special service requests, frequent flyer linkage, etc.), enabling relying parties to cross check boarding pass data against airline systems and the traveller’s EU issued identity credentials to ensure consistency and authenticity; the framework therefore allows a traveller to prove identity and entitlement in a way that preserves authenticity, interoperability and operational efficiency across European jurisdictions while reducing fraud and operational friction.
