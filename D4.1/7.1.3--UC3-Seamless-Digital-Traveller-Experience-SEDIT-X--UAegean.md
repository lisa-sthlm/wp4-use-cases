### UC 3: Seamless Digital Traveller Experience (SEDIT-X) (UAegean)

SEDIT-X is designed to align with the role model and interaction patterns of the EUDI Wallet Architecture and Reference Framework (ARF). It applies the standard EUDI Wallet relationship between issuer, wallet/holder, and verifier/relying party across the airport, ferry, hospitality, and university episodes, while using an EUDIW Intermediary Service to simplify integration with domain systems. In this sense, SEDIT-X does not define a separate architecture; it applies the ARF model in a cross-sector travel scenario.

#### ARF baseline used for SEDIT-X

For the purposes of SEDIT-X, the architectural baseline should be understood as the **EUDI Wallet ARF v2.8.0**, which provides the reference role model for Wallet Units, PID Providers, Attestation Providers, Relying Parties, and Intermediaries, and frames the ecosystem as one in which users obtain attestations from trusted issuers and present them to relying parties in a privacy-preserving manner. This baseline statement is used here as the reference point for mapping the SEDIT-X implementation roles and flows. As the ARF continues to evolve, later iterations may refine details, but the current SEDIT-X design is already structurally aligned with the v2.8.0 role model and interaction logic.

#### ARF components used in SEDIT-X

**Wallet Instance / Wallet Unit**

SEDIT-X assumes that the traveller uses a conformant **EUDI Wallet instance** on a personal device under the traveller's control. The wallet stores the PID and the additional journey credentials used across the different episodes, receives credential offers, authenticates where required by the issuance profile, and constructs verifiable presentations for later disclosure to service providers. WP4 does not develop the wallet itself; rather, it consumes wallet instances provided by the broader EUDI Wallet ecosystem.

In SEDIT-X, the wallet instance is the common user-facing interaction channel across domains. It is used to:

- receive credentials such as boarding passes, ferry boarding credentials, booking reference credentials, student credentials, accessibility attestations, and vehicle-related credentials;
- present credentials and selectively disclosed attributes to verifiers;
- support user consent and authentication before disclosure; and
- in selected scenarios, support payment authorisation and proximity interactions.

**PID Provider**

The **PID Provider** is not implemented by SEDIT-X itself, but is assumed as an external foundational ARF actor. The traveller is expected to hold a valid **PID** in the wallet before the use case begins. In SEDIT-X, the PID functions as the high-assurance identity anchor used to bootstrap trust and, where needed, bind or support the issuance and verification of sector-specific credentials.

The PID Provider therefore participates indirectly but critically in SEDIT-X. Its output is consumed in several episode flows, especially where:

- identity proofing is required before issuing additional journey credentials;
- identity attributes must be presented during booking or check-in;
- boarding or travel entitlements must be linked to the same subject as the PID; or
- a relying party needs high-assurance identity evidence as part of a selective-disclosure presentation.

#### Attestation Providers

SEDIT-X uses multiple **Attestation Providers**, depending on the episode and credential type. These are the ARF actors that issue the non-PID attestations used throughout the traveller journey. In SEDIT-X, attestation-provider functions may be performed by different domain actors, including:

- **airline or airport-side issuer functions**, for example when issuing a boarding pass credential or an airport-specific biometric QR credential;
- **ferry operator or participating travel agency** functions, when issuing a ferry boarding credential;
- **booking, DMC, or hotel-side issuer functions**, when issuing a booking reference credential for hospitality flows;
- **university or alliance-side issuer functions**, when issuing student or academic access credentials; and
- **competent authorities or recognised organisations**, when issuing accessibility/disability-related attestations.

In implementation terms, some of these issuer roles may be technically exposed through the **EUDIW Intermediary Service**, but conceptually they remain ARF attestation-provider functions: they issue attestations to the traveller's wallet through standard issuance flows.

#### Relying Party / Verifier

The SEDIT-X service providers that request data from the wallet and make operational decisions based on the result correspond to the ARF **Relying Party** role. Across the different episodes, this role is played by, for example:

- airport and airline operational systems at check-in, baggage-drop, security, and gate;
- ferry boarding and port-side verification systems;
- hotel booking portals, self-service check-in terminals, and hotel/PMS-connected systems;
- university service providers that verify student or alliance credentials; and
- mobility or payment-related service endpoints where wallet evidence is required.

These relying parties prepare presentation requests, receive the verifiable presentation from the wallet, validate trust, integrity, freshness, and status, and then return a business decision to the connected operational system.

#### EUDIW Intermediary Service as ARF Intermediary

A distinctive feature of SEDIT-X is the use of an **EUDIW Intermediary Service**. This maps naturally to the ARF notion of an **Intermediary**, i.e. a specialised actor operating on behalf of relying parties and, in some cases, issuer-side business systems, to handle the complexity of wallet interactions. In the airport material, the intermediary is explicitly framed as the integration layer that centralises issuance, verification, trust checks, status checks, eligibility checks, and biometric orchestration rather than duplicating wallet logic at each touchpoint.

In practical terms, the intermediary in SEDIT-X:

- exposes APIs to domain systems such as airport touchpoints, booking portals, hotel terminals, and other service backends;
- translates between wallet protocols and domain-specific operational APIs;
- centralises issuance and verification operations;
- applies trust and policy checks consistently; and
- reduces implementation burden for sector systems that are not natively wallet-aware.

Accordingly, the intermediary should be described in the deliverable as an **ARF-aligned intermediary / special class of relying-party-side integration actor**, not as a replacement for the wallet, PID provider, or attestation provider roles.

**Protocol profiles used in SEDIT-X**

SEDIT-X is based on the standard wallet protocol pattern already reflected in the uploaded materials.

#### OpenID4VCI 1.0 for issuance

Credential issuance in SEDIT-X follows an **OpenID for Verifiable Credential Issuance (OpenID4VCI) 1.0-style** model. This is used whenever a sector system determines that a new credential should be issued to the traveller's wallet, such as:

- a **boarding pass credential** in the airport episode;
- a **biometric QR credential** in the airport BioQRCode path;
- a **ferry boarding credential** in the ferry episode; and
- a **booking reference credential** in the hospitality episode.

#### OpenID4VP 1.0 for verification / presentation

Credential presentation in SEDIT-X follows an **OpenID4VP 1.0-style** model. This is the common pattern used across airport, ferry, hotel, and university verification moments.

The standard presentation sequence is:

1. a verifier / relying party prepares a presentation request;
2. the request is delivered to the wallet via an engagement mechanism;
3. the wallet shows the requested credentials or attributes and the purpose of the request;
4. the user approves disclosure;
5. the wallet returns a verifiable presentation; and
6. the verifier validates trust, integrity, credential status, freshness, and policy satisfaction before returning an operational decision.

This OpenID4VP-based pattern is the architectural default for SEDIT-X verification flows.

#### Engagement and transport patterns

Within the OpenID4VP model, SEDIT-X uses multiple engagement patterns depending on the operational setting:

- **QR code, redirect, or deep-link engagement** for remote or browser-supported flows, such as hotel booking and some issuance or check-in interactions;
- **proximity interaction** for operational touchpoints such as airport and ferry scenarios; and
- in the airport episode specifically, a **proximity verification path via NFC**, described as aligned with **ISO/IEC 18013-5-style proximity interaction** for direct wallet presentation.
