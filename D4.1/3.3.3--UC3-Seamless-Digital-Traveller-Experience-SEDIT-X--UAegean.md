### UC 3 (SEDIT-X)

The development of the **SEDIT-X** use case has been informed by a combination of **EU digital identity reference materials**, **sector-specific standards and guidance**.

##### Key reference documents

The main reference sources used for SEDIT-X are the following:

1. **EUDI Wallet Architecture and Reference Framework (ARF)**  
   The ARF is the main architectural baseline for the wallet ecosystem and for the roles used across SEDIT-X, including the wallet instance, PID Provider, Attestation Provider, Relying Party, and Intermediary patterns. For the current specification work, SEDIT-X has been aligned with the **ARF v2.8.0** baseline.
   - European Digital Identity Wallet – Architecture and Reference Framework v2.8.0: <https://eu-digital-identity-wallet.github.io/eudi-doc-architecture-and-reference-framework/2.8.0/architecture-and-reference-framework-main/>

2. **eIDAS 2.0 and the EUDI Wallet Implementing Acts**  
   SEDIT-X is framed within the legal and technical requirements of the European Digital Identity Wallet framework under **eIDAS 2.0**, including the Implementing Regulations that define wallet requirements, integrity, core functionalities, and related technical specifications.
   - Regulation (EU) 2024/1183 on the European Digital Identity Framework: <https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024R1183>
   - Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/2979: <https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024R2979>
   - Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/2980: <https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024R2980>

3. **ICAO standards and guidance for digital travel credentials and machine-readable travel documents**  
   For the airport-facing parts of SEDIT-X, ICAO material is relevant as the broader aviation baseline for digital travel credentials, document authenticity, and travel document data structures.
   - ICAO Doc 9303 – Machine Readable Travel Documents: <https://www.icao.int/sites/default/files/publications/DocSeries/9303_p1_cons_en.pdf>
   - ICAO guidance on Digital Travel Credentials (DTC): <https://www.icao.int/sites/default/files/TRIP/Publications/High-Level-Guidance-explaining-ICAO-DTC.pdf>

4. **IATA specifications and guidance**  
   For airport operations and seamless passenger processing, SEDIT-X also draws on IATA work around **One ID**, digital identity, contactless travel, and biometric-enabled passenger processing.
   - IATA One ID: <https://www.iata.org/en/programs/passenger/one-id/>
   - IATA Digital Identity Program: <https://www.iata.org/en/programs/innovation/digital-identity/>

5. **European Student Card Initiative (ESCI) documentation**  
   For the university and student-mobility dimension of SEDIT-X, the European Student Card Initiative provides the main European reference point. This is particularly relevant for the academic-access episode and for the handling of student-related attestations and cross-border higher-education interactions.
   - European Student Card Initiative: <https://erasmus-plus.ec.europa.eu/european-student-card-initiative>
   - European Student Card: <https://erasmus-plus.ec.europa.eu/european-student-card-initiative/card>
   - Erasmus Without Paper: <https://erasmus-plus.ec.europa.eu/european-student-card-initiative/ewp>
   - European Student Card General Specifications: <https://erasmus-plus.ec.europa.eu/resources-and-tools/documents-and-guidelines/european-student-card-general-specifications>
