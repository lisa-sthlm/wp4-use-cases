## Terminology and Definitions

This section defines the most frequently encountered acronyms and concepts used throughout D4.1. The full glossary, including terms used only in specific chapters, is provided in **@sec-Annex_C**.

The terms below are grouped by topic for readability. A term in **bold** is the primary form used in the deliverable; alternative forms or sources are given in parentheses.

### EUDIW ecosystem and eIDAS 2.0 core terms

* **EUDIW (European Digital Identity Wallet).** The wallet defined by Regulation (EU) 2024/1183 (eIDAS 2.0), which every Member State is required to provide to its citizens. The EUDIW holds, manages, and presents identity attributes and electronic attestations of attributes. WP4 consumes EUDIW instances produced by participating Member States; it does not develop the wallet itself.
* **eIDAS / eIDAS 2.0.** The Regulation establishing the framework for electronic identification and trust services in the Union. *eIDAS 1.0* refers to Regulation (EU) 910/2014; *eIDAS 2.0* refers to Regulation (EU) 2024/1183, which amends Regulation 910/2014 and introduces the EUDIW. Section 6.1 covers the eIDAS 2.0 framework in detail.
* **Wallet Holder.** The natural person who carries the EUDIW and uses it to hold, manage, and present credentials. In WP4, wallet holders take various profiles depending on the use case: business travellers, tourists, hotel guests, students, event attendees, and travellers with reduced mobility.
* **Wallet Provider.** The Member State entity (or its delegate) that provides the EUDIW to citizens. The wallet provider is responsible for wallet activation, for the security of the wallet instance, and for the interfaces to the PID provider and to attestation providers.
* **PID (Person Identification Data).** The foundational identity credential issued into the EUDIW by or on behalf of a national authority, at the highest level of assurance, as mandated by Article 5b of eIDAS 2.0. Every WP4 use case that verifies the identity of a wallet holder relies on a PID.
* **PID Provider.** The national authority, or its delegate, that issues the PID into the wallet.
* **Supervisory Body.** The national authority responsible, under eIDAS 2.0, for the supervision of the wallet provider, of the PID provider, and of qualified trust service providers in its Member State.

### Credentials and attestations

* **Credential.** A generic term for a digitally signed statement that an issuer makes about a subject and that the subject can present from the wallet to a relying party. In WP4, credentials are either PIDs or EAAs (qualified or not), with the special case of DTCs handled by WP3.
* **EAA (Electronic Attestation of Attributes).** A digitally signed attestation by an issuer about one or more attributes of the wallet holder. Most sector-specific WP4 credentials (boarding passes, hotel booking confirmations, student credentials, event tickets) are issued as non-qualified EAAs.
* **QEAA (Qualified Electronic Attestation of Attributes).** A qualified variant of the EAA, issued by a qualified trust service provider under stricter requirements, and carrying legal effect equivalent to a paper attestation. WP4 uses QEAAs in the small number of cases where qualified status is required.
* **DTC (Digital Travel Credential).** A passport-derived credential, defined under ICAO Doc 9303 and integrated into the EUDIW ecosystem through the work of WP3. WP4 consumes DTCs at touchpoints where passport-level assurance is required (notably UC 1, UC 2, UC 9, and the relevant parts of UC 3).
* **CI (Credential Issuer).** The organisation that issues a credential into the wallet. In WP4, credential issuers include airlines, rail and ferry operators, urban-mobility providers, hotels, online travel agencies, student-card issuers (such as the GYSC), the ERUA Alliance, universities, and event organisers.
* **RP (Relying Party, also: Verifier).** The organisation that verifies a credential presented from the wallet, in order to grant access to a service. In WP4, relying parties include airports, airlines at the gate, train operators, ferry boarding points, hotel front desks, campus access systems, stadium entry systems, and certain public authorities.
* **Trust Service Provider.** The entity that provides trust services relied on by other actors, such as qualified certificates for electronic signatures and seals, qualified timestamps, and the qualified preservation of electronic signatures.
* **Trusted-List Operator.** The entity that operates the registration machinery and publishes the trusted lists of PID providers, attestation providers, and relying parties under Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/2980.

### Technical standards and protocols

* **ARF (Architecture and Reference Framework).** The architectural reference document for the EUDIW, maintained by the eIDAS Expert Group. WP4 implementations align with the ARF profiles selected by WP2.
* **OpenID4VCI.** The OpenID Foundation specification for *Verifiable Credential Issuance*. WP4 uses OpenID4VCI for the issuance flows.
* **OpenID4VP.** The OpenID Foundation specification for *Verifiable Presentation*. WP4 uses OpenID4VP for the verification flows at relying parties.
* **mdoc.** The mobile-document credential format defined by ISO/IEC 18013-5. WP4 uses mdoc for proximity (in-person) verification flows, such as airport gates, hotel kiosks, campus turnstiles, and ferry boarding lanes.
* **SD-JWT VC (Selective Disclosure JSON Web Token Verifiable Credential).** The JSON-based credential format that supports selective disclosure natively. WP4 uses SD-JWT VC for remote, web-mediated flows.
* **Selective disclosure.** The capability of the wallet to release only the attributes strictly necessary for a given relying-party request, rather than the full credential. Selective disclosure is mandated by eIDAS 2.0 and is exercised by every WP4 use case.
* **Unobservability (or unlinkability).** The property by which the wallet provider cannot track which relying parties the wallet holder interacts with. Mandated by eIDAS 2.0 and inherited by WP4.

### APTITUDE project structure

* **APTITUDE.** The Large Scale Pilot project (call DIGITAL-2024-BESTUSE-TECH-06) that delivers WP4 and seven other Work Packages. APTITUDE is a Member-State-driven initiative supporting the 2026 EUDIW deployment target.
* **WP (Work Package).** APTITUDE is organised into eight Work Packages (WP1 to WP8). Section 2.3 places WP4 within the full set.
* **WP4.** *Tickets and check-in*, the Work Package that produces this deliverable. Led by GRNET S.A.
* **UC (Use Case).** WP4 comprises 11 accepted use cases, numbered UC 1 through UC 11. Each UC has a designated lead partner (or pair of co-leads for UC 2 and UC 6).
* **Scenario.** A coherent end-to-end user journey. WP4 defines three scenarios: Streamlined Travel (S1), Hotel Check-In (S2), and Student Access (S3).
* **LSP (Large Scale Pilot).** A category of EU-funded projects that pilot the EUDIW. APTITUDE is one of several LSPs; sister LSPs frequently referenced include DC4EU, POTENTIAL, EWC, and NOBID.

### Regulatory and governance terms

* **Implementing Acts.** The implementing legislation adopted by the European Commission to operationalise eIDAS 2.0. The first comprehensive set was adopted in late 2024. Section 6.1 lists the Implementing Acts directly relevant to WP4.
* **GDPR.** Regulation (EU) 2016/679, the General Data Protection Regulation. Applies to all WP4 personal-data processing.
* **NIS2.** Directive (EU) 2022/2555, the cybersecurity directive that applies to essential and important entities, including transport operators and digital infrastructure providers.
* **AI Act.** Regulation (EU) 2024/1689, applicable to AI systems used for biometric identification (relevant to UC 9 and UC 3 Episode 1).
* **PSD2.** Directive (EU) 2015/2366, the Payment Services Directive 2, applicable at the payment touchpoints of WP4 use cases.
* **SCA (Strong Customer Authentication).** The PSD2 requirement for multi-factor authentication of certain payment transactions. WP4 inherits SCA obligations through its hand-off to WP6.
* **DPIA (Data Protection Impact Assessment).** The Article 35 GDPR assessment that applies to processing operations likely to result in a high risk to data subjects. DPIAs for WP4 use cases are produced under WP7.
* **ETIAS, EES, APIS.** Border-control instruments referenced in Section 6.2.2: ETIAS (Regulation (EU) 2018/1240) is the European Travel Information and Authorisation System; EES (Regulation (EU) 2017/2226) is the Entry/Exit System; APIS refers to the Advance Passenger Information regime (Regulations (EU) 2025/12 and 2025/13).

### Sector-specific terms most frequently used

* **IATA (International Air Transport Association)** and **ICAO (International Civil Aviation Organization).** The two international bodies whose specifications frame the air-travel use cases of WP4. Notably, ICAO Doc 9303 specifies machine-readable travel documents and underpins the DTC.
* **OSDM (Open Sales and Distribution Model).** The European rail industry specification for ticket distribution, relevant to UC 7 and UC 8.
* **ISIC / GYSC.** The International Student Identity Card, issued by the Global Youth and Student Community (GYSC). Used as an EAA in UC 6, UC 7, and Episode 5 of UC 3.
* **ERUA Alliance Card.** The credential of the European Reform University Alliance, used in UC 6 and Episode 5 of UC 3.
* **ERASMUS.** The Union programme for education, training, youth, and sport, governed by Regulation (EU) 2021/817. It is the policy frame for the cross-border student-mobility use cases.
* **ESCI (European Student Card Initiative).** The Commission-coordinated initiative that frames the interoperability of student cards across the Union. Referenced in Section 6.2.3 as a key reference for the student-mobility UCs.
