#### UC 3: Seamless Digital Traveller Experience (SEDIT-X) (UAegean)

Within **UC3: Seamless Digital Traveller Experience (SEDIT-X)**, the contribution of **WP6** focuses on those parts of the traveller journey where the **EUDI Wallet** is used not only for identity and entitlement verification, but also for **payment authorisation**. In SEDIT-X, payment is not treated as a standalone generic use case. Instead, it is embedded in specific operational moments of the cross-domain traveller journey where a user must complete a regulated payment interaction in order to continue or complete the service flow.

For the current UC3 scope, the payment dimension of SEDIT-X is centred on **two concrete scenarios** and one **optional scenario**:

1. **airport excess-baggage payments**, where the traveller is asked to pay an additional fee during airport processing; and
2. **ferry-ticket payments**, including cases where the ferry booking also involves a **vehicle**, in which case the **mVRC** is relevant to the transaction context and booking evidence.
3. **Taxi payments** (optional). Subject to adequate support from PSPs, taxi passengers may be able to pay using their **EUDI Wallet**, through either **card-based** or **account-to-account** payment flows, following the relevant WP6 specifications.

In both cases, the EUDI Wallet acts as the **user-facing authorisation channel**, while the operational systems involved in the airport or ferry environment consume the outcome of the wallet-based payment flow through the relevant integration path. This means that SEDIT-X does not position the wallet as replacing airline, ferry, merchant, or PSP systems. Rather, the wallet is used to support a more trusted and user-controlled way to perform payment authorisation in a context where identity, entitlement, and transaction details are already part of the service journey.

At a high level, the SEDIT-X payment logic follows the broader design principle used across the use case: the traveller interacts with the wallet, the wallet discloses or proves only the data needed for the specific step, the payment authorisation is bound to the transaction context, and the resulting payment evidence is propagated to the service-side ecosystem so that the relevant operational decision can be taken. In the airport case, that decision concerns whether baggage drop-off can proceed after excess-baggage charging. In the ferry case, it concerns whether the ticket purchase and related booking flow can be completed successfully.

**Role of payments within SEDIT-X**

The objective of introducing wallet-based payment flows into SEDIT-X is to demonstrate that the EUDI Wallet can support not only traveller identification and entitlement verification, but also selected **payment-enabled service interactions** that are tightly linked to the traveller journey.All work is based, designed and dependant on the results of  **WP6**. This is important because, in practice, real travel processes often combine:

- identity verification,
- booking or entitlement validation,
- transaction authorisation, and
- a final operational decision such as check-in, boarding, or service fulfilment.

The SEDIT-X payment perspective therefore explores how these interactions can remain coherent from the traveller’s point of view. Instead of switching from a wallet-based identity or entitlement step into a completely separate user interaction model, the traveller may continue to use the wallet as the trusted channel through which payment is authorised and cryptographically linked to the relevant transaction context.

In this sense, WP6 contributes to SEDIT-X by adding the **payment and banking layer** needed to make selected traveller flows more complete and closer to real operational deployment.

**Payment scenario 1: Airport excess-baggage payment**

The first SEDIT-X payment scenario concerns the case where a traveller reaches an airport processing point, typically **check-in** or **baggage drop-off**, and the baggage is found to exceed the permitted allowance. In this situation, an **additional payment** is required before the baggage can be accepted and the traveller can continue the journey.

 
**Payment scenario 2: Ferry-ticket payment, including vehicle transport**

The second SEDIT-X payment scenario concerns **ferry-ticket booking and purchase**, including the more specific case where the booking also includes a **vehicle**. In this part of the use case, the traveller uses the wallet not only in connection with travel entitlement and later boarding validation, but also at the point where the ferry booking is purchased.

At a high level, the ferry-payment scenario is intended to support a booking flow in which the wallet-mediated payment step is linked to the ticket-purchase context. After successful booking and payment, a **Ferry Boarding Credential** is expected to be issued to the traveller’s wallet for later use in the ferry episode.
