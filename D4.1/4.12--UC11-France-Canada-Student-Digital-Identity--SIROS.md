## UC 11: France-Canada Student Digital Identity (SIROS Foundation)

**4.12.1 Summary**

UC 11 pilots the technical integration between the EU digital-identity ecosystem and the Canadian digital-identity ecosystem, with a first focus on the Quebec region. A French student uses the European Digital Identity Wallet (EUDIW) and credentials issued in the EU — a Personal Identification Data (PID) credential and a student Electronic Attestation of Attributes (EAA) — to access services offered by Canadian relying parties. The initial relying-party scope covers universities and student housing, with possible extensions to government services and student discounts on public transport.


**4.12.2 User story**

"As a French student travelling to the Quebec region to study, I want to use my EUDIW to show that I am a French national, that I am a student in France, and that I will be a student in Canada, so that I can enrol at the university, enrol in student housing, and access student discounts using my wallet."


**4.12.3 Actors (summary)**

User (French student); PID Issuer (French government via ANTS); Credential Issuer / EAA (GYSC); Relying Parties on the Canadian side (university, student-housing provider, and possibly transit operators and government services); Canadian-side coordination (DIACC); regional partner (Quebec government, via the ANTS–Quebec cooperation arrangement).


**4.12.4 Main flow**

Marie authenticates to a Canadian university and/or student-housing portal by presenting her PID via the EUDIW, establishing French nationality.
Where EU student status is required (for example at student housing), Marie presents her GYSC student credential through the EUDIW.
The relying party verifies the credentials and completes the enrolment step; fallback to paper is available where a credential cannot be parsed or validated.


**4.12.5 Success criteria and KPIs**
* Canadian relying parties correctly interpret the PID as evidence of French nationality and correctly validate the GYSC student credential.
* Technical integration of the EUDIW with the Canadian-side verifier is achieved.
* KPI targets: ≥ 3 cross-border transactions; ≥ 2 EU wallets in use; ≥ 1 PID and ≥ 2 EAAs in scope (one may be a PUBEAA).
