## UC 1: Board the Flight (Amadeus)


The **Board the Flight** use case (UC) contributes directly to **Scenario 1: Streamlined Travel Experience**, by demonstrating how **digital credentials stored in the EUDI Wallet (EUDIW)** can be used seamlessly across **online** and **airport** touchpoints, reducing friction while maintaining regulatory compliance.

This use case is also **closely related to WP3**, as it relies on **Digital Travel Credentials (DTC)** as the primary digital credential for non‑EU travel scenarios.



The **Board the Flight** use case (UC) addresses this scenario through two consecutive and complementary episodes.

---

### Reference Example for illustration purpose only 
**Route:** Helsinki – Heathrow (Non‑EU)

**Digital Credential Used:** Digital Travel Credential (DTC)

**Wallet:** EUDI Wallet (EUDIW)

---

### Episode 1 – Online Flow (SSCI) / Collect and Verify API at Online Check‑In

**Context:**  
During **Self‑Service Online Check‑In (SSCI)**, prior to departure.

**Description:**  
The traveler uses the **EUDI Wallet** to share a **Digital Travel Credential (DTC)** during the online check‑in process. The airline collects and verifies **Advance Passenger Information (API)** required for non‑EU travel.

**Key Aspects:**
- Demonstrates how the **EUDI Wallet can be used in online flows**
- DTC is shared **remotely**, without physical presence
- Supports early compliance with border and carrier obligations
- Enables **pre‑travel identity and document verification**

**Outcome:**  
Advance passenger and identity verification is completed **before arrival at the airport**, reducing later friction.

---

### Episode 2 – Airport Flow (Boarding) / Board the Flight at the Airport

**Traditionally**, at boarding time, travelers are required to present **both a boarding pass and a physical identity document** to the airline agent in order to complete identity and entitlement checks.

In **Episode 2**, **Amadeus proposes to test a user‑centric boarding approach**, aligned with **IATA One ID principles** and **EUDI Wallet (EUDIW) adoption**, with the objective of **simplifying and facilitating the passenger experience**.

**Key highlights of the proposed approach:**
- ✅ No need to download additional applications such as airport or airline applications → **EUDI Wallet acts as the single trusted travel companion**. 
- ✅ No pre‑registration required→  **DTC acts as the single trusted digital credentials**. 
- ✅ No physical presentation of a boarding pass or identity document → **EUDI Wallet is your pass**. 
- ✅ No storage of any biometrics →   **1:1 verification at boarding time**. 

 
The identity and travel verification performed during online check‑in (Episode 1) is **securely re-used at the airport** during boarding.

The traveler simply presents the **EUDIW**, enabling a **1:1 identity verification model** that:
- Ensures the required level of **security and regulatory compliance**
- **Significantly reduces friction** at the boarding gate
- Delivers a **seamless and intuitive passenger experience**

**Description:**  
The traveler presents the **same DTC stored in the EUDI Wallet** at boarding time, the same DTC was previously used in online check-in.

   A **1:1 identity verification** is performed to ensure:
- The traveler is the rightful holder of the DTC e.g the person presenting the credential matches the portrait data (DTC DG2)


A **airline back-end verification** is performed to ensure:
- The traveler has a valid boarding pass and DTC DG1 matches the API (advance passenger information) in the backe-end booking record


**Key Aspects:**
- Demonstrates **re-use of the EUDI Wallet at the airport**
- Supports **identity checks** at boarding
- Aligns with non‑EU regulatory requirements
- Ensures continuity between online and airport processes

**Outcome:**  
Boarding is completed with a trusted digital credential, maintaining security while streamlining passenger flow.
