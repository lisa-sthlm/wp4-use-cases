#### Electronic Attestations of Attributes (EAA / QEAA)

In the **Board the Flight** use case, the **Digital Travel Credential (DTC)** remains the **main digital credential**, as it is valid for **both EU and non‑EU travel**.

In this context, the DTC is compliant with **ICAO DTC Type 1 or Type 2** definitions. It provides authoritative, cross‑border‑recognized identity data suitable for regulated air‑travel processes.

According to the **WCO–IATA–ICAO Guidelines on Advance Passenger Information (API)** the following **core data elements**, typically derived from the **Machine Readable Zone (MRZ)** of the official travel document, **may be required by Border Control Authorities**, provided that there is a **legal basis** for aircraft operators to collect and transmit this information (https://www.icao.int/sites/default/files/TRIP/Publications/WCO-IATA-ICAO-API-Guidelines.pdf)

Data elements listed in this paragraph may be required by Border Control Agencies, provided there is a legal basis for aircraft operators to collect and transfer the information:
- **Travel Document Number**  
  (Passport number or other official travel document number)

- **Issuing State or Organization of the Official Travel Document**  
  (State or organization responsible for issuing the official travel document)

- **Official Travel Document Type**  
  (Indicator identifying the type of official travel document)

- **Expiration Date of Official Travel Document**  
  (Expiration date of the official travel document)

- **Surname / Given Name(s)**  
  (Family name and given name(s) of the holder as they appear on the official travel document)

- **Nationality**  
  (Nationality of the holder)

- **Date of Birth**  
  (Date of birth of the holder)

- **Sex**  
  (Sex of the holder)


These elements are commonly used for **API transmission** and are derived directly from standardized MRZ fields

**DTC data groups**

The DTC used in this use case includes the following ICAO data groups:

**DG1(Data Group 1)** contains the **core biographic and travel document attributes** of the traveler.  
It corresponds to data typically derived from the **MRZ of an official travel document** and is used for **identity verification and regulatory checks**.

- DG1 is primarily used for **Advance Passenger Information (API)** collection

**DG2 (Data Group 2)**  contains the **biometric reference data** of the traveler, as defined by ICAO. Typical DG2 attributes include facial image (biometric reference)

DG2 is used for:
- **Biometric identity verification**
- **Automated or contactless processes**, such as biometric boarding at the airport
- Binding biometric verification to an existing DG1‑based identity

**Usage across episodes**

- **Episode 1 – Collect and verify Advance Passenger Information (API)**  
  During **Self‑Service Online Check‑In (SSCI)**, the traveler shares a *digital credential* stored in the **EUDI Wallet**.  
  In this episode, **DG1 is used** to remotely collect and verify API data.

- **Episode 2 – Board the flight at the airport**  
  At the airport, the traveler shares a *digital credential* stored in the **EUDI Wallet**.  
  In this episode, **DG1 and DG2 are used** to support identity verification and, where applicable, biometric boarding.
