### UC 7: Accessing Discounting Train Fares via EUDIW (PagoPA)

While the UC7 operational core lies in the **Verification phase**, the overall reliability of the system also implies the Issuance and Revocation phases. This integration between the national issuer (IPZS) and the service provider (Trenitalia) ensures a frictionless user experience based on international standards, ensuring that only valid and up-to-date credentials benefit from dedicated services.

Specifically, UC7 pilots the remote verification of the PID and the European Disability Card to enable personalized fares and discounts within the Trenitalia booking system. At its heart, the scenario focuses on the seamless sharing of these credentials via the EUDI Wallet, in full compliance with OpenID4VP standards and the principle of Selective Disclosure.

Given the scope of this UC, the **issuance** of the credentials by IPZS is considered a precondition; the testing scenario assumes that passengers have already provisioned their verifiable attestations within their wallet. Similarly, while **revocation** is not the primary focus of the pilot, it remains an essential component of the lifecycle, as the system must perform background checks to guarantee the administrative validity of the credentials presented during the booking process.
