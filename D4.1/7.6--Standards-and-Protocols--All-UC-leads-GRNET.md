## Standards and Protocols

This section lists the technical standards and protocols used across the WP4 use cases. Its purpose is to fix, in one place, the normative references that the per-UC subsections (7.6.1 through 7.6.11) rely on, and to make the dependency on WP2 profiles explicit. The standards are introduced briefly here; the authoritative profile versions for the pilot are those selected by WP2 (@sec-2.3.1) and reported in its technical profile documentation.

The standards used across WP4 fall into four categories.

* **EUDIW core protocols and credential formats**, defined by the OpenID Foundation, ISO/IEC, and the IETF, and applied across all 11 use cases.
* **Trust framework standards**, defined by ETSI and applied through the trusted-list machinery established by Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/2980.
* **Sector-specific standards**, defined by sectoral bodies (ICAO, IATA, the European rail industry, the European Student Card Initiative) and applied at the touchpoints where WP4 use cases interact with sector systems.
* **Cryptographic primitives and serialisation standards**, applied beneath the protocols above.

**EUDIW core protocols and credential formats**

The EUDIW core protocols and credential formats are exercised by every WP4 use case. They are introduced at the architectural level in Section 7.1 and at the credential level in Section 7.2; the present subsection lists them by their normative reference.

* **OpenID4VCI** (OpenID Foundation, OpenID for Verifiable Credential Issuance). Used for the issuance flows between attestation providers and the wallet instance.
* **OpenID4VP** (OpenID Foundation, OpenID for Verifiable Presentation). Used for the presentation flows between the wallet instance and relying parties, in both remote and proximity bindings.
* **SIOPv2** (OpenID Foundation, Self-Issued OpenID Provider v2). Used together with OpenID4VP at presentation touchpoints that require self-issued identifiers, where the WP2 profile mandates it.
* **ISO/IEC 18013-5** (mobile driving licence, mdoc). Used for the proximity verification flows: airport gates, hotel check-in kiosks, campus turnstiles, stadium entries, train carriages, and ferry boarding lanes.
* **ISO/IEC 23220** series (mobile eID building blocks). Referenced where the ISO/IEC 18013-5 ecosystem extends beyond the driving-licence use case to general mobile identity, in line with the WP2 profile.
* **SD-JWT VC** (IETF, Selective Disclosure JSON Web Token Verifiable Credential). Used for web-mediated and mobile-app remote flows.
* **W3C Verifiable Credentials Data Model**. Used as the conceptual reference for credential semantics, where applicable, alongside the concrete serialisations above.

**Trust framework standards**

The trust framework standards govern how WP4 relying parties evaluate the trustworthiness of presented credentials. They are described from the consumption side in Section 7.5; the present subsection lists them by their normative reference.

* **ETSI TS 119 612** (Trusted lists). The format and operational rules of the trusted lists published by the trusted-list operators (Section 7.5).
* **ETSI EN 319 401, EN 319 411, EN 319 412** (Policy and security requirements for trust service providers; certificate profiles). Apply to the qualified trust service providers whose certificates underpin the signatures on PIDs, EAAs, and QEAAs.
* **X.509** (ITU-T) and **PKIX** (IETF RFC 5280). The certificate framework used by the trust services above.
* **Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/2980** is the legal instrument that operationalises these standards within the eIDAS 2.0 trust framework. WP4 applies the standards through this Implementing Regulation rather than directly.

**Sector-specific standards**

WP4 use cases interact with sector-specific systems, which carry their own normative references. The standards listed below are those that the consolidated list of regulations (@sec-6.2) and the UC specifications identify as applicable.

* **ICAO Doc 9303** (Machine Readable Travel Documents). Applies to the DTC consumed by WP4 air-travel use cases (UC 1, UC 2, UC 9, and Episode 1 of UC 3).
* **ICAO Annex 9** (Facilitation). Provides the international context for the API/APIS regime and for travel-document processes.
* **API and iAPI data elements** (joint ICAO/IATA/WCO guidelines). The technical data specification for advance passenger information, applied at the carrier-to-authority interface in UC 1 and UC 9.
* **IATA NDC** (New Distribution Capability) and the IATA passenger-processing resolutions. Apply to the airline-side flows of UC 1 and UC 4, and to the ETA flow of UC 2 where the airline performs an at-boarding ETA verification.
* **OSDM** (Open Sales and Distribution Model). Apply to the rail-ticketing flows of UC 7, UC 8, and Episode 2 of UC 3 where rail operators are involved. Note that the rail passenger rights legal framework is currently a gap in the consolidated list (see @sec-6.2.2 and @sec-10.2).
* **European Student Card Initiative (ESCI)** specifications and the **European Student Identifier (ESI)**. Apply to UC 6 and Episode 5 of UC 3, and inform UC 11 in the FR side. ESCI is currently a gap in the consolidated list and is recorded for closure in @sec-10.2.
* **ERUA Alliance technical guidance**. Applies to the ERUA Alliance Card flows in UC 6 and Episode 5 of UC 3.
* **ISO/IEC 19794** (Biometric data interchange) and **ISO/IEC 30107** (Presentation attack detection). Apply to UC 9 and the biometric episode of UC 3.
* **National hospitality and police-reporting APIs**. The APIs exposed by the national guest-registration systems referenced in @sec-6.2.1 (national instruments still to be inventoried). They apply at the hotel touchpoint in UC 3 (Ep. 4), UC 8, and UC 10.

**Cryptographic primitives and serialisation standards**

Beneath the protocols and credential formats above, WP4 uses a small set of cryptographic primitives and serialisation standards.

* **COSE** (CBOR Object Signing and Encryption, IETF RFC 9052/9053). The signature envelope used by mdoc credentials in line with ISO/IEC 18013-5.
* **JWS, JWT, JWE** (IETF RFC 7515, 7519, 7516). The signature, token, and encryption envelopes used by SD-JWT VC and by OpenID4VCI/OpenID4VP messages.
* **CBOR** (IETF RFC 8949) and **JSON** (IETF RFC 8259). The serialisation formats underpinning the credential envelopes above.
* **ECDSA** (FIPS 186-5) and **EdDSA** (IETF RFC 8032), together with the curves selected by the WP2 profile, as the signature schemes for issuer and wallet keys.

**Profile versioning and the WP2 reference**

The standards above are listed by name; the precise version of each, as profiled by WP2, is reported separately in WP2's technical profile documentation.

Sections **7.6.1** to **7.6.11** record, for each UC, the precise standards and profile versions implemented, together with any sector-specific standard that applies only to that UC.
