## Sources and Tools

This section documents the **reference documents** and the **collaborative tools** that supported the stock-taking phase of WP4. 

#### Categories of sources

The reference documents consulted during the stock-taking phase fall into four broad categories.

**Regulatory sources.** These are the primary legal instruments that constrain every WP4 use case. The main entries are the eIDAS 2.0 Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2024/1183), the Implementing Acts adopted in late 2024 that operationalise the Regulation, the GDPR, and the sector-specific regulations covered in Chapter 6 (such as national hotel guest-registration laws, EU passenger rights regulations for air, rail, and maritime transport, and the rules governing student mobility).

**Technical standards and specifications.** These are the normative documents that describe how the EUDIW and its surrounding ecosystem work at the technical level. The most important entries are the **Architecture and Reference Framework (ARF)** maintained by the eIDAS Expert Group, the **OpenID4VCI** and **OpenID4VP** protocol specifications. 

**Project-internal sources.** These are the documents produced inside APTITUDE on which WP4 builds. The Grant Agreement itself is the foundational reference, with its objectives, scenarios, tasks, and deliverables. The outputs of WP2 (technical profiles and testbed) provide the operational framework for conformance testing. The stock-taking deliverables of WP3, WP5, and WP6 (D3.1, D5.1, D6.1) are companion documents that share the same methodological phase and that capture interfaces with WP4 at their hand-off points. Outputs from earlier EU Large Scale Pilots (such as DC4EU, POTENTIAL, EWC, and NOBID) are potential sources of consultation.

**Domain-specific documentation.** These are the documents that describe the organisational and operational context of each sector. 

#### Categories of tools

The collaborative tools used during the stock-taking phase fall into three categories.

**Document repositories and authoring tools.** The deliverable is authored in Markdown and stored in a shared GitHub repository, which serves as the single source of truth for the deliverable's content. The repository is used for version control, for pull-request-based review, and for issue tracking. The same repository hosts the Quarto configuration (`_quarto.yml`) that assembles the individual Markdown files into the HTML and PDF rendered versions of D4.1. Diagrams embedded in the deliverable are authored in Mermaid, following the standards documented in Section 3.4.1; this choice allows the diagrams to be reviewed as text alongside the prose they illustrate.

**Shared templates.** Two templates, designed by the WP4 coordinator, structured the stock-taking work. The first is the lightweight **UC metadata spreadsheet** described in Section 3.1, used during the early consolidation from 28 candidates to 11 accepted use cases. The second is the **UC specification template**, documented in detail in Section 3.4 and filled by every UC lead to produce the UC specification sheets that appear in Annex A.

**Communication and coordination tools.** Throughout the stock-taking phase, coordination between the WP4 coordinator, the UC leads, and the partner organisations was carried out through a combination of scheduled coordination calls (both bilateral and scenario-focused), written questionnaires circulated to UC leads for specific topics, and targeted workshops where the agenda called for synchronous discussion with multiple partners. The stakeholders consulted through these mechanisms are documented in Section 3.2, which complements the source inventory presented here.

#### Per-UC sources and tools

Because each of the 11 use cases operates in a different sector and engages a different set of partner organisations, the specific sources and tools that were most relevant vary significantly from one UC to another. For this reason, the inventory of sources and tools presented above is complemented by 11 per-UC subsections. Each per-UC subsection is authored by the corresponding UC lead, who is best placed to know which references actually shaped the analysis of the use case. Where a source or tool is common to several UCs (for example, the ARF or the UC specification template), the per-UC subsection simply refers back to the shared inventory above rather than repeating the description. The authoritative reference list for the deliverable as a whole is compiled, from all of these sources, in **Annex E (References)**.
